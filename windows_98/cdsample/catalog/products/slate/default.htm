Slate
Slate is the interactive magazine of news, politics, and culture edited by Michael Kinsley. Kinsley is the former co-host of CNN's "Crossfire" and longtime editor and columnist for the New Republic.

Slate online magazine combines top quality insight and analysis with the interactive and multimedia potential of the World Wide Web to inform and entertain. The magazine brings to the Web America's leading journalists, policy makers, and cultural critics. Slate's articles, reviews, and forums take full advantage of Internet technology by using animation, audio and video clips, and interactive discussions.
Slate
  • The opening section of Slate includes short, timely articles about politics, news, entertainment, and trends, as well as thought-provoking opinion pieces.
  • Today’s Papers - Daily (six days a week) summary and instant analysis of the lead stories in five major U.S. newspapers. Sign up to get daily e-mail delivery!
  • The Week/The Spin - Examines how the media have viewed the week's big news events
  • In Other Magazines - Tracks the content of the leading print publications
  • Dispatches and Dialogues - Less formal, but equally insightful, these e-mail style missives provide a more personal view of individuals, issues, and the events that shape them.
  • Back of the Book - Features reviews of books, movies, music, and theater, other critical essays, poems (read aloud by their authors), and, coming soon, a weekly puzzle
  • The Fray - The magazine's reader forum, where thousands of Slate readers share thoughts and opinions on topics addressed by the magazine

Mungo Park™
Mungo Park online adventure magazine delivers multimedia, interactive adventure travel reports and high-caliber writing to the Internet's World Wide Web

Mungo Park is the world's first online interactive adventure travel magazine to feature real exploration.It's a pioneering, interactive adventure travel experience. Headed by Richard Bangs, award-winning author, multimedia producer, and founding partner of America's oldest adventure travel company (Mountain Travel-Sobek), this Web-zine features interactive expeditions, articles by well-known, best-selling authors, and compelling editorial columns.
Mungo Park
  • Interactive Expeditions - Mungo Park was kicked off in September with a descent of the Tekeze River in Ethiopia, part of the deepest gorge in Africa and one of the world's last great unexplored territories. On future expeditions, members will view the solar eclipse in Siberia, retrace the steps of explorer Mungo Park down the Niger river to Timbuktu, and more.
  • At the Park - Captures the community of Mungo Park through live chats. Threaded conferencing allows members to exchange comments and insights on topics of interest. -
  • Wild Lit - Features monthly contributions from best-selling authors who set out on journeys and pen essays about their adventure travels exclusively for Mungo Park. Authors include T. Coraghessan Boyle, author of the definitive novel on Mungo Park; Tom Robbins, author of Even Cowgirls Get the Blues; Bill Broyles, screenwriter of the movie Apollo 13; and Tama Janowitz, author of Slaves of New York. -
  • Drum - Includes regular columns from well-known journalists including Virginia Morell, author of Ancestral Passions, a chronicle of the Leakey family; Franz Lidz, staff writer for Sports Illustrated; plus rich, timely editorial content from Mungo Park's own editorial staff.
